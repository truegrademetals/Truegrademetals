Engineering overview

Introduction

Nickel alloys are chosen when stainless steels no longer meet the demands of temperature, corrosion or mechanical stress. The four families most commonly specified — Monel, Inconel, Incoloy and Hastelloy — each occupy a distinct performance space.

This guide compares those families side-by-side, lists common international grade equivalents, and gives application-based selection recommendations. Use it to narrow your specification before requesting a quotation.

When to use this guide

  • Comparing alloy families — understand the trade-offs between Monel, Inconel, Incoloy and Hastelloy for your environment.
  • Specifying a grade — map temperature, corrosion and mechanical requirements to a proven alloy and international standard.
  • Preparing an RFQ — identify the product form, size range and certification level you need before requesting a quote.

Performance data

Corrosion resistance

All four alloy families outperform austenitic stainless steels in chloride-containing or strongly acidic environments, but their relative performance differs by medium.

Chloride pitting

Hastelloy C-276 > Inconel 625 > Incoloy 825 > Monel 400. Higher molybdenum and chromium improve chloride resistance.

Oxidising acids

Hastelloy C-22 and C-276 perform well in nitric and mixed acids. Inconel 625 is preferred for oxidising/cyclic conditions.

Reducing acids

Monel 400 and Hastelloy B-3 resist hydrochloric and sulphuric acids where oxidising species are low.

Common questions

Engineering FAQs

What is the most corrosion-resistant nickel alloy?

Hastelloy C-276 is widely specified for its broad corrosion resistance, including wet chlorine, hypochlorite and strong oxidisers. For more severe mixed acids, Hastelloy C-22 may be preferred.

Which nickel alloy is best for high-temperature service?

Inconel 625 offers excellent oxidation resistance up to about 980 °C. Inconel 718 is preferred when high strength is required up to about 700 °C.

Can I get a Mill Test Certificate with my order?

Yes. Mill Test Certificates (MTC) to EN 10204 3.1 or 3.2 are supplied as standard, including chemical composition, mechanical properties and heat-treatment records.
