  1. TrueGrade Metals

Overview

ASTM B166 is a standard for nickel alloy / superalloy bar and rod. It includes many very common alloy materials. In this article, we will extract the noteworthy requirements in ASTM B166 for you in detail, which will allow you to better control the quality in product procurement and inspection.

Grades & Chemical Composition

Grades

Inconel 600 Inconel 601 Inconel 602CA Inconel 617 Inconel 690 Inconel 693 UNS N06045 UNS N06603 UNS N06696

The main scope of ASTM B166 is nickel-chromium-iron alloys. Among them, the common materials are: Inconel 600 (UNS N06600), Inconel 601 (UNS N06601), Inconel 602CA (UNS N06025), Inconel 690 (UNS N06690) and Inconel 693 (UNS N06693). The two compositions of nickel and chromium bring resistance to various corrosions to the alloy. Additionally, the standard includes a nickel-chromium-cobalt-molybdenum alloy, Inconel 617 (UNS N06617). In this alloy, cobalt and molybdenum elements improve the solid solution strengthening effect of the alloy, allowing the alloy to obtain higher strength.

ASTM B166 mainly includes three products: rod, bar and wire.

Among them, Bars are divided into round bar, square bar, flat bar and hexagon bar according to the cross-sectional shape. Large size bars are generally produced by hot rolling process. Smaller sizes of bars are produced by cold drawing. Among them, the small size round bar is also called rod.

Wires are generally produced by cold drawing. Smaller in size and continuous in length. Wires can more finely control the properties of the alloy through the combination of cold drawing and heat treatment.

Delivery State

For bars and rods, most alloys are delivered in the annealed condition. The alloy in this state has lower strength but better toughness.

For Inconel 600, Inconel 690 and Inconel 693, the standard also specifies the status of hot-worked and cold worked. These two states retain more work hardening, giving the material higher strength. Among them, the strength of the cold-worked state material can reach the maximum.

For wires, the standard specifies the different states of Inconel 600 and Inconel 690. Like bars, wires also have annealed and cold-worked states. In addition, wires also have the status of spring temper. This product is also known as spring wire. It is mainly used to make springs and has extreme tensile properties.

Certification

The supplier shall provide the material test certificate corresponding to the material when delivering the goods. The certificate should list customer information, supplier information, standards, heat number, product form, delivery status, size, chemical composition, mechanical properties and other necessary information in detail.

Conclusion

ASTM B166 mainly contains some commonly used nickel-chromium-iron alloys and nickel-chromium-cobalt-molybdenum alloys. Most of the alloys in the standard have excellent corrosion resistance. The products in ASTM B166 are mainly bars, rods and wires. For some grades, different delivery states are specified in the standard, please fully confirm with the supplier before placing an order.
